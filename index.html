About me

My interest in IT started when I finished my work experience in year 10 as an IT assistant. Simples stuff such as downloading drivers, disassembling and assembling desktop PC parts. My interest grew even further when my friend from university introduced me to Linux as a free operating system when I was a student studying engineering. But I felt I was not studying the right course when I started to play around with Linux on an old pc.

I dropped out of engineering due to mental health issues. Worked 2 years before going back to studies to figure out what I wanted to do. My sister was the one that mentioned I spend most of my time with computers. So, I end up trying out a diploma of IT at VU to see if I like IT. From my studies in diploma in IT, I see that I have strengths/likes and weaknesses/dislikes in IT. end up hating the theory in computer networking but love the configuration of live Linux servers (AWS).

I started to 3 build gaming because I love playing games in my spare time when I do not have friends to spend time with. I also spend time fixing android phones and laptops. One of the satisfying things when fixing computers/laptops and android phones is that they can function again from a dead/nonfunction state.

Why did I choose RMIT?

I choose RMIT because I would like to find like minded people (independent learners) and different mentality of studying. Felt the other university was copy and paste but stop the students from using their own brain to troubleshoot programming problems.

I do not like spoon feed as I noticed how bad students cannot think for themselves once they quickly ask for help when they didn’t try solving their own problem or look up for answer online forms. Felt that bullying is common at my other university. So, I avoid it all together.

What I expect from my studies with RMIT?

That I will grow with others and gain valuable materials from studying with RMIT. Also, I avoided networking courses because I didn’t like it as much as physically connecting a lot of cables to servers based on a schematic diagram.
